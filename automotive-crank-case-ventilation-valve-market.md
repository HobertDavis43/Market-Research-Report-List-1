<p><p>The automotive crankcase ventilation valve market includes two primary types: plunger type and diaphragm type. Plunger type valves operate using a mechanical plunger that opens and closes to regulate airflow, effectively controlling pressures in the crankcase. In contrast, diaphragm type valves utilize a flexible diaphragm that responds to pressure changes, ensuring consistent ventilation. Both types are essential for maintaining engine efficiency, reducing emissions, and preventing oil leakage, catering to varying automotive designs and performance requirements across the industry.</p></p>
