<p><p>Lavender essential oil is categorized into several types based on the species of lavender. Lavandin, a hybrid, is known for its high yield and strong aroma, making it popular in commercial products. Lavender Highland, from higher altitudes, offers a sweet, floral scent, sought after for therapeutic uses. Lavender Stoechas has a distinct, spicy fragrance and is often used in potpourri. Lavender Spike, with its camphoraceous notes, is favored in medicinal applications. Other types include various regional and specialty oils, catering to diverse consumer preferences.</p></p>
