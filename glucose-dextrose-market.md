<p><p>The glucose (dextrose) market primarily comprises two types: D-Glucose Monohydrate and Anhydrous Glucose. D-Glucose Monohydrate contains water molecules, making it more soluble, commonly used in food and pharmaceuticals. Anhydrous Glucose, devoid of water, offers a higher concentration of glucose, often utilized in production processes and as a calorie source in nutritional products. Both forms serve essential roles across various sectors, including food processing, pharmaceuticals, and biotechnology, catering to diverse consumer needs and applications.</p></p>
