<p><p>The humeral implant market can be categorized into fixed and variable implants. Fixed implants offer a stable and permanent solution for shoulder surgeries, designed for long-term use with a predetermined size and shape, typically used in straightforward anatomical reconstructions. In contrast, variable implants provide adjustable options, enabling personalized fitting for diverse patient anatomies and surgical requirements. This flexibility enhances surgical outcomes and allows for better alignment and stability during postoperative recovery, catering to a wider range of clinical scenarios.</p></p>
