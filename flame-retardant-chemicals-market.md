<p><p>The flame retardant chemicals market is primarily classified into two types: organic and inorganic flame retardant chemicals. Organic flame retardants, typically derived from carbon-based compounds, include materials like brominated and phosphorus-based substances, known for their effectiveness in various applications such as textiles and plastics. In contrast, inorganic flame retardants are composed of mineral-based compounds, such as aluminum hydroxide and magnesium hydroxide, which work by releasing water vapor when heated, effectively cooling and retarding combustion. Both types play vital roles in enhancing fire safety across multiple industries.</p></p>
