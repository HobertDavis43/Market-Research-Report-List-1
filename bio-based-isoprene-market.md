<p><p>The bio-based isoprene market comprises two primary production methods: microbial fermentation and chemical conversion. In contrast, chemical conversion involves synthesizing isoprene from bio-based feedstocks through chemical processes, often resulting in higher yields. Both methods aim to provide a renewable alternative to traditional isoprene production, which primarily relies on fossil fuels, catering to the growing demand for sustainable materials in various industries.</p></p>
