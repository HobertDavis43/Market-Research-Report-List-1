<p><p>Ultraviolet light sensors are categorized into three types based on their wavelength ranges: UVA, UVB, and UVC. UVA sensors detect wavelengths from 320 to 400 nm, primarily used in applications like sun exposure monitoring. UVB sensors cover 280 to 320 nm, crucial for assessing skin damage and sun safety. UVC sensors, which range from 100 to 280 nm, are primarily utilized in disinfection applications, as they can effectively eliminate harmful microorganisms. Each type serves distinct purposes in health, safety, and environmental monitoring.</p></p>
