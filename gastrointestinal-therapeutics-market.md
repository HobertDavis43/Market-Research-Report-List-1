<p><p>The gastrointestinal therapeutics market encompasses two primary delivery methods: oral and parenteral. Oral administration involves medications taken by mouth, convenient for patients and suitable for chronic conditions such as irritable bowel syndrome. In contrast, parenteral delivery includes injections or infusions, offering rapid absorption and effectiveness for severe gastrointestinal disorders. Parenteral methods are often used when oral medications are not viable, such as in cases of vomiting or malabsorption, ensuring critical therapies reach the bloodstream efficiently.</p></p>
