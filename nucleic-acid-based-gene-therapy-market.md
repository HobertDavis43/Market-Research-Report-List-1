<p><ul><li>Anti-Sence and Anti-Gene</li><li>Short Inhibitory Sequences</li><li>Gene Transfer Therapy</li><li>Nucleoside Analogs</li><li>Ribozymes</li><li>Aptamers</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Nucleic Acid Based Gene Therapy encompasses various market types targeting genetic disorders. Anti-sense and anti-gene therapies inhibit gene expression by binding to RNA or DNA. Short inhibitory sequences, like siRNA, disrupt specific mRNA, preventing protein production. Gene transfer therapy involves delivering genetic material to cells to correct defects. Nucleoside analogs mimic natural nucleotides, affecting DNA/RNA synthesis. Ribozymes function as RNA enzymes to cleave specific RNA targets, while aptamers bind to proteins, blocking their function. Other therapies include innovative delivery methods and emerging technologies.</p></p>
