<p><p>The pharmaceutical ampoules market is primarily categorized into two materials: glass and plastic. Glass ampoules are favored for their chemical resistance, inertness, and ability to preserve sensitive pharmaceutical substances, providing a barrier against contamination and ensuring stability. On the other hand, plastic ampoules offer advantages such as lightweight, shatter resistance, and lower production costs. While glass remains the preferred choice for many products requiring sterility, plastic is gaining traction for its flexibility and ease of handling in various applications.</p></p>
