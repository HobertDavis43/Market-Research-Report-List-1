<p><p>The medical device connectivity market is divided into two primary types: wireless and wired. Wireless connectivity includes technologies such as Bluetooth, Wi-Fi, and cellular networks, enabling devices to communicate without physical connections, offering flexibility and ease of integration within healthcare settings. Conversely, the wired market involves direct physical connections (like USB, Ethernet, or serial ports), ensuring reliable data transfer with minimal interference. Both types are essential for efficient data management, remote monitoring, and enhancing patient care in healthcare environments.</p></p>
