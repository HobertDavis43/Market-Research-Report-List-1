<p><p>Corrosion inhibitors in water treatment are classified into two main types: organic and inorganic. Organic inhibitors, typically comprised of organic compounds, function by forming protective films on metal surfaces, reducing metal ion dissolution and preventing corrosion. Inorganic inhibitors, on the other hand, are usually mineral-based and work through mechanisms like cathodic protection or passivation. Both types are crucial in various industrial applications, helping to extend equipment lifespan and minimize maintenance costs by mitigating the effects of corrosion in water systems.</p></p>
