<p><strong>The OBD Interface Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>OBD-I</li><li>OBD-II</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The OBD (On-Board Diagnostics) interface market is primarily divided into two types: OBD-I and OBD-II. OBD-I, introduced in the early 1980s, provided basic diagnostic capabilities and varied across manufacturers, leading to inconsistency. In contrast, OBD-II, mandated in the mid-1990s, standardized diagnostic protocols, enabling comprehensive vehicle monitoring and troubleshooting. OBD-II supports more advanced features, such as real-time data access and enhanced emissions control, making it widely adopted in modern vehicles, thereby dominating the market today.</p></p>
