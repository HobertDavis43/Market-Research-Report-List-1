<p><p>The biological polymer coatings market encompasses three primary types: polysaccharides, proteins, and lipid compounds. Polysaccharides, such as cellulose and chitosan, offer biocompatibility and biodegradability, making them ideal for food and biomedical applications. Proteins, like gelatin and silk fibroin, provide excellent mechanical properties and can enhance adhesion and stability. Lipid compounds, including phospholipids and fatty acids, are utilized for their barrier properties and ability to interact with biological environments. These coatings are increasingly used in medical devices, pharmaceuticals, and food packaging.</p></p>
