<p><p>Gaming motherboards come in different form factors, primarily BTX and ATX, each catering to specific needs. The ATX (Advanced Technology eXtended) type is the most popular, offering versatility with numerous expansion slots and better cooling options, ideal for high-performance gaming rigs. BTX (Balanced Technology eXtended) is designed for improved airflow and thermal management, but is less common. Its layout reduces heat buildup, though limited motherboard options can restrict component choices for gamers seeking the latest technology.</p></p>
